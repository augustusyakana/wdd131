Puppies

Golden Retriever Puppy

🐾 Fun Fact: Golden Retrievers are natural swimmers! Their water-repellent coats and webbed feet make them excellent at retrieving from lakes and rivers — hence the name!

Beagle Puppy

🐾 Fun Fact: Beagles have over 220 million scent receptors in their noses (humans have about 5 million), which makes them scent-tracking pros. This is why they're often used at airports to sniff out contraband!

Corgi Puppy

🐾 Fun Fact: Corgis were bred to herd cattle — those little legs are fast and nimble enough to nip at cow ankles and dodge kicks. Also, their short stature helps them duck under hooves!

Dachshund Puppy

🐾 Fun Fact: Dachshunds were originally bred to hunt badgers! Their name in German literally means “badger dog,” and their long, narrow bodies helped them burrow into badger dens.

Boston Terrier Puppy

🐾 Fun Fact: Known as the “American Gentleman,” Boston Terriers were the first dog breed developed in the United States. Their tuxedo-like coat and polite manners earned them the nickname.

Tricolor Beagle Puppy

🐾 Fun Fact: Beagles love company! They're pack animals and tend to do best in environments where they can be around other dogs or humans. A lonely Beagle will likely get creative (and a little mischievous) to keep entertained.
